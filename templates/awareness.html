What Just Happened

You received a simulated phishing email designed to look like a legitimate message. You clicked a link in that email, which in a real attack could have led to credential theft, malware installation, or financial fraud.

Red Flags to Watch For

Sender domain mismatch — Check that the "From" email matches the real company domain exactly.
Urgency & pressure — Phrases like "immediate action required" or tight deadlines are classic manipulation tactics.
Unexpected requests — Unusual wire transfers, password resets, or benefit updates out of the blue should raise suspicion.
Hover before you click — Hovering over links reveals the real destination URL before you commit.

✅  Next time: When in doubt, verify through a separate channel — call the sender directly using a known number, or forward the email to your IT/security team before clicking anything.
